What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers and servers that enables communication and data exchange. It provides the infrastructure for various services, including email, file sharing, and the Web. The Internet allows people to connect and share information across the world, making it an essential part of modern life.

Key Features of the Internet

  • Global Connectivity: The Internet connects millions of devices worldwide, allowing people to communicate and share information regardless of their location.
  • Decentralization: The Internet is a decentralized network, meaning that there is no central authority controlling it. This allows for greater freedom and innovation.
  • Scalability: The Internet can accommodate a vast number of users and devices, making it a scalable platform for communication and information sharing.
  • Interoperability: The Internet supports a wide range of protocols and technologies, allowing different devices and applications to communicate with each other seamlessly.
